Frequency Response Services

Frequency response services are used to manage frequency in the British power market. Frequency changes in line with the second-by-second balance between system demand and generation. If demand is greater than generation, the frequency falls while if generation is greater than demand, the frequency rises. Frequency must be kept between 49.5Hz and 50.5Hz, but the system has operational limits of 49.8Hz and 50.2Hz.

Legacy Services

Frequency response has a long history in the British power market and historically response was provided by Firm Frequency Response (FFR, Dynamic and Static) plus Mandatory Frequency Response (MFR). They were designed based on the operational capabilities of large thermal units, running at baseload (or at least long runs), supported by more flexible pumped storage assets.

As the renewable contribution to generation has increased and battery storage has entered the market, ways of procuring frequency response and the capabilities of units have changed. At the same time, less large thermal generation on the market has meant less conventional generator s have been available to provide MFR (Mandatory Response) on demand. National Grid is therefore transitioning away from FFR and MFR to new services that suit a fuel mix with more renewable and battery capacity

Dynamic FFR

The Dynamic Firm Frequency Response (FFR) service is used to deliver continuous response that acts based upon on-going changes in frequency in the market, rather than being triggered at a set frequency deviation from 50.0Hz (as Static FFR is). This response is split into the Primary, Secondary and High sub-services. Primary and Secondary increase power and so raise frequency, whilst High reduces power and so decreases frequency. Primary reacts within 10 seconds sustained for 20 seconds, Secondary within 30 seconds and sustained for 30 minutes and High within 10 seconds, sustained indefinitely.

Dynamic FFR is procured in monthly tenders. From 2019, these were month-ahead only, though previous long-term tenders have secured some volume out to March 2021.

Static FFR

The Static Firm Frequency Response (FFR) service is used to deliver 30 second upwards (frequency increase) response that acts as a low-cost source of longer-term frequency management. The system only needs to maintain frequency within certain ranges, so this service is a large part of the whole frequency response service. This service can be provided by diesel plants and other reciprocating units.

Static FFR is procured in monthly tenders. From 2019, these were month-ahead only.

Mandatory Frequency Response (MFR)

Mandatory Frequency Response (MFR) is a service that must be provided by all large generators in the market, under their Mandatory Framework Agreements, which are put in place when they connect to the network. Unlike Firm Frequency Response, these generators are not required to provide this service over a pre-agreed period, but instead are used as required, if they are online.

Under this service, generators will operate as usual, but where frequency response is required, they will deliver it alongside their planned generation. Where they need to be moved into position (normally backing a plant down or in an extreme case bringing a plant online) this will be achieved via normal Balancing Mechanism volumes.

Recently Introduced Services (2017-2020)

National Grid has developed three services to try and better suit the capabilities of units now available for frequency response. These are Enhanced Frequency Response, Dynamic Low High and Low Frequency Static. Each ended up being 'trial' or interim services, as through their activity more was learned about frequency response from batteries.

Enhanced Frequency Response was the first of these services. It was procured only once, in 2016, for four years.

Dynamic Low High and Low Frequency Static are currently active and procured through the weekly frequency response auction trial.

Dynamic Low High (DLH)

DLH (Dynamic Low High) is a dynamic service that delivers equal volumes of Primary, Secondary and High frequency response. It is a dynamic service procured to maintain system frequency close to 50Hz and is nearly identical to Dynamic FFR, the main difference being that the latter can deliver unequal volumes of Primary, Secondary and High frequency response. Any units that are currently tested for the monthly Dynamic FFR service are able to provide DLH without re-testing. The minimum volume requirement is 1MW and full output must be delivered within 10 seconds for Primary and High, and 30 seconds for Secondary. DLH is one of the two services procured in the Phase 2 Auction Trial from November 2019 to the present, the other being LFS. Auctions are held weekly, and tender documents are also made available weekly.

Low Frequency Static (LFS)

LFS (Low Frequency Static) is a static service procured to maintain system frequency close to 50Hz and is triggered when frequency drops to 49.6Hz. It is a faster static service compared to the Static FFR service. The minimum volume requirement is 1MW and full output must be delivered within 1 second. LFS was the service procured in the Phase 1 Auction Trial from June to November 2019 and is currently procured in the Phase 2 Auction Trial from November 2019 to the present. Auctions are held weekly, and tender documents are also made available weekly.

Enhanced Frequency Response (EFR)

EFR was procured in one tender round, in 2016, for operation from 2017, for four years. Due to the speed and activity required, it is only suitable for battery units. EFR operates before Primary Dynamic FFR or MFR, i.e. at a sub 1-second timescale. This means it corrects smaller frequency deviations, and as such is most comparable with Dynamic response, which also responds to changes in frequency below 0.2Hz.

EFR provision runs from April 2017 to February 2022, with the greatest volume of provision (200MW) from March 2018 to March 2021, inclusive.

Newest Frequency Response Services (2020->)

Dynamic Containment, Dynamic Moderation and Dynamic Regulation have been designed to be the future range of frequency response services, replacing Firm Frequency Response and the interim services (EFR, DLH and LFS).

The three new services should be stackable with each other. All will be procured day-ahead, as National Grid moves to closer to real-time procurement of ancillary services.

Dynamic Containment came into operation in October 2020.

Dynamic Containment (DC)

Dynamic Containment is a fast-acting post-fault service to contain frequency within the statutory range of +/- 0.5Hz in the event of a sudden demand or generation loss. The service delivers very quickly and proportionally to frequency but is only active when frequency moves outside of operational limits (+/- 0.2Hz). Full delivery must be within 1 second, but no faster than 0.5 seconds. The dead band for DC is +/- 0.015Hz.

Dynamic Moderation

Dynamic Moderation will be a pre-fault service, acting occasionally to back up the continuous Dynamic Regulation service. It will be symmetrical, with response within 1 second, lasting for 20 minutes.

Dynamic Regulation

Dynamic Regulation will also be a pre-fault service, acting continuously with a 2 second lag, to provide continuous small corrections when the frequency is close to 50.0Hz.
